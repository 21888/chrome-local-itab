## Design Context

### Users
Local iTab is for Chrome users who want a private, fast, locally controlled new tab page. They open it many times per day to search, launch saved sites, and make small adjustments without handing data to a remote service.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, precise, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a durable browser tool: calm enough for daily use, clear enough for first-time users, and restrained enough for public release.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained utility aesthetic. Prefer tinted neutrals, one muted accent, strong alignment, predictable spacing, and crisp controls. Avoid heavy glassmorphism, neon glow, blue-purple AI gradients, decorative motion, and large empty cards.

### Design Principles
- Local-first is the product promise; privacy and network state must stay explicit.
- Search and shortcuts are the primary workflow; optional cards stay visually secondary.
- Every setting needs clear scope, immediate feedback, and a safe recovery path.
- Components should be compact, readable, keyboard-friendly, and stable across viewport sizes.
- Public-release quality means no console errors, no unclear permissions, no hidden network behavior, and no broken empty states.
